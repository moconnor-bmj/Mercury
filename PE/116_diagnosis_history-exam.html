History and exam

            
Key diagnostic factors

            

               

                  

                     

                        

                           

                              
                              common
                              
                              VIEW ALL
                              
                              
                              
                           

                        

                        

                        

                           

                              
dyspnoea

                           

                        

                        

                           

                                 
Dyspnoea is the most common presenting symptom and is seen in 50% of patients with PE. [ESC] [NICE] 

                                    
    
                                    
  • May be acute and severe in central PE but is often mild and may be transient in small peripheral PE. [ESC]
    • 
                                    
  • Worsening dyspnoea may be the only symptom in patients with pre-existing heart failure or pulmonary disease. [ESC]
    • 
                                    
  • Tachypnoea (respiratory rate ≥20 breaths per minute) is a common presenting sign in patients with acute PE (21% to 39%). [POLLACK]
    • 
                                    
  • Symptoms of PE usually come on acutely rather than gradually
    • 
                                    

                                 
                           

                        


                        

                           

                              
chest pain

                           

                        

                        

                           

                                 
Pleuritic chest pain is most common and seen at presentation in 39% of patients with PE. [ESC][NICE]

                                 
    
                                 
  • The pain is normally localised to one side of the chest and is unlikely to be central. This is usually caused by pleural irritation due to distal emboli causing pulmonary infarction. [STEIN] 
    • 
                                 
  • Normally acute onset.
    • 
                                 

                                 
Central PE may also present with angina-like chest pain but this is uncommon. [ESC]

                                 
    
                                 
  • This possibly reflects right ventricular (RV) ischaemia and requires differential diagnosis with acute coronary syndrome (ACS) or aortic dissection. [ESC]
    • 
                                 

                                 

                                    

                                       

                                          
pan_tool PRACTICAL TIP/PITFALL PANEL

                                          
Do not use chest pain on palpation of the chest wall to distinguish musculoskeletal chest pain from PE. [CP]

                                          
    
                                             
  • This sign is present in 20% of people with PE.
    • 
                                          

                                       

                                    

                                 

                           

                        


                        

                           

                              
Signs of concurrent deep vein thrombosis

                           

                        

                        

                           

                                 
Typically pain and swelling in one leg, although both legs may be affected.[NICEDVT]

                                 
    
                                 
  • Examine the lower limbs for tenderness, changes to skin colour and temperature, and venous distension. [NICEDVT]
    • 
                                 

                                 
    
                                 
  • Features of concurrent DVT are present in 24% of patients with a diagnosed PE. [ESC]
    • 
                                 

                           

                        



                     


                  

               

            


            
Risk factors


            

               

                 

                     
Your history should check for the following risk factors to help inform your assessment of the likelihood of PE and to determine whether any subsequently confirmed PE is provoked or unprovoked: [ESC][NICE][] [POMP] [CROLES] [ORTEL]

                     
    
                        
  • No provoking cause can be found in 30% of patients. [ESC]
    • 
                     

                     

                           

                             
                               

                                 Past medical history
                                 Strong risk factors
                               

                             
                             
                               

                                 
                                 
Active cancer

                                    
    
                                    
  • The highest relative risk of PE is in patients with multiple myeloma, brain and pancreatic cancer
    • 
                                    
  • The largest numbers of episodes of PE are in patients with lung, colon and prostate cancer. [FREYZEK]
    • 
                                    
  • Active cancer is present in 22% of patients with confirmed PE. [GOLD]
    • 
                                    

                                    
Recent surgery or hospitalization (last 3 months)

                                    
    
                                    
  • Present in 29% of patients with confirmed PE. [GOLD]
    • 
                                    
  • Lower limb fractures and joint replacements are strong provoking factors for PE. [ESC]
    • 
                                    

                                    
Previous or current DVT

                                    
    
                                    
  • Present in 25% of patients with confirmed PE. [GOLD]
    • 
                                    

                                    
Pregnancy and 6 weeks postpartum [ESC][TOWER][POMP]

                                    
    
                                    
  • There is a more than 4-fold increased risk of thrombosis throughout pregnancy.
    • 
                                    
  • The increased risk of PE is even higher in the postnatal period - up to 60 times higher in the 3 months after delivery, compared with the risk in non-pregnant women
    • 
                                    

                               

                             
                           

                           

                                 
                                   

                                      
                                     Weak risk factors
                                   

                                 
                                 
                                   

                                     
                                     
                                          
Other significant medical comorbidities

                                          
    
                                          
  • For example, congestive heart failure, COPD, concurrent sepsis. [GOLD]
    • 
                                          

                                          
Obesity

                                          
    
                                          
  • BMI ≥ 29 kg/m2
    • 
                                          

                                          
Increasing age

                                          
Varicose veins

                                          
Known thrombophilias

                                          
    
                                          
  • Factor V Leiden mutation; prothrombin G20210A mutation; antithrombin deficiency; protein C deficiency; protein S deficiency; antiphospholipid antibody syndrome. [CROLES] [ORTEL]
    • 
                                          

                                          
Other

                                          
    
                                          
  • Central vein catheter
    • 
                                          
  • Nephrotic syndrome,
    • 
                                          
  • Chronic dialysis
    • 
                                          
  • Myeloproliferative disorders
    • 
                                          
  • Paroxysmal nocturnal
    • 
                                          
  • Haemoglobinuria or Behcets disease
    • 
                                          
  • Blood transfusion
    • 
                                          

                                     		
                                   

                                 
                           

                           

                                 
                                   

                                       Social history
                                       Strong risk factors
                                   

                                 
                                 
                                   

                                     
                                     
                                          
History of immobilisation

                                             
    
                                             
  • >5 days of bed rest present in 28% of patients with confirmed PE. [GOLD]
    • 
                                             

                                     		
                                   

                                 
                           

                           

                                 
                                   

                                      
                                     Weak risk factors
                                   

                                 
                                 
                                   

                                     
                                     
                                          
Long-distance travel

                                          
Smoking

                                     		
                                   

                                 
                           

                           

                                 
                                   

                                       Drug history
                                       Weak risk factors
                                   

                                 
                                 
                                   

                                     
                                     
                                          
Combined oral contraceptive pill 

                                          
    
                                          
  • The risk of VTE associated with different COCs is influenced by the progestogen type. COCs containing levonorgestrel, norethisterone, or norgestimate have the lowest risk (5-7 events per 10,000 women per year), whereas those containing drospirenone, desogestrel, or gestodene have the highest risk (9-12 events per 10,000 women per year). [EMA]
    • 
                                          
  • Progestogen-only methods of contraception do not appear to be associated with an increased risk of VTE. [FAMP]
    • 
                                          

                                          
Hormone replacement therapy [NICE]

                                          
    
                                          
  • There is a small increased risk of PE or DVT associated with use of oral HRT but no increased risk from transdermal HRT at standard doses. [NG23]
    • 
                                          

                                     		
                                   

                                 
                           

                           

                                 
                                   

                                       Family history
                                       Weak risk factors
                                   

                                 
                                 
                                   

                                     
                                     
                                          
First-degree relative with a history of confirmed PE or DVT. [NICE]
